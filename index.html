About

Not Your Typical Path.
Exactly By Design.

I grew up in Fort Collins, Colorado before my family moved to Chicago — where hockey became everything. Playing for Indiana Ice in the USHL, getting drafted 3rd overall, and winning a Clark Cup didn't just shape me as a player. It taught me how to compete, how to lead, and how to build something worth being part of.

West Point came next. I captained the Division I hockey program, finished in the top 17% of my class, and left with a systems engineering degree and a commission. Six years flying Black Hawks and commanding people and missions across the Asia-Pacific followed.

Now I'm in the business world, and the same things that made me effective on the ice and in the Army are making me effective here. At Oply, I've generated over 50% of company revenue and expanded our reach from 13 to 48 states. The tools are different. The mentality isn't.

What I'm building toward is the intersection of sports, private equity, and technology — because I've lived the athlete experience at an elite level, operated in high-stakes environments, and know how to build from scratch. I'm here to acquire the technical skills and foundational knowledge to work at that intersection.
